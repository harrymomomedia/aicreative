# Ad Copy Formats

Vary copy structure, not only nouns and hooks. Keep the campaign's approved facts constant while
changing how the reader discovers them.

## Workflow

1. Load the active campaign/domain skill and approved copy.
2. Separate fixed facts from creative choices.
3. Select formats that suit the visual and messenger.
4. Vary opening mechanism, point of view, evidence order, paragraph rhythm, and CTA framing.
5. Draft headline and primary text together.
6. Audit the batch for repeated sentence skeletons.
7. Obtain verbatim approval before creating ad-platform copy rows.

Campaign-specific claims, locations, qualification rules, testimonials, attorney text, and
disclaimers do not belong in this skill.

## Format Menu

| Format | Structure | Useful For |
|---|---|---|
| Micro | Two or three short lines | Fast native creative |
| First-person advertorial | Discovery story in one voice | Testimonial or UGC |
| Journalistic explainer | Context, evidence, implication | News or document creative |
| Open letter | Direct address to one reader | Emotional portrait |
| FAQ | Real objections answered | Q&A or interface creative |
| Myth versus fact | Misbelief followed by correction | Educational ads |
| Listicle | Numbered points | Checklists and diagrams |
| Timeline | Then, turning point, now | Change or precedent |
| Pure prose | Emotional narrative without bullets | Quiet visual creative |
| Checklist-led | Opens on conditions or signs | Eligibility or product fit |
| Dialogue | Two voices reveal the offer | Text thread or podcast |
| Classic direct response | Qualifier, benefits, CTA | Use sparingly |
| PAS | Problem, agitation, solution | Pain-aware audiences |
| Fact hook | One verified fact, then relevance | Educational proof |
| Second-person mini-story | Reader becomes protagonist | Relatable scenarios |
| Conditional cascade | Repeated `If...` lines | Layered audience fit |
| Objection cascade | Anticipate and answer resistance | High-friction offers |
| Quote stack | Several attributed or clearly illustrative voices | Social proof |

## Batch Diversity

- Do not use one format more than a few times in a large batch.
- Avoid repeating the same first sentence with synonyms.
- Give long-form, medium, and micro copy distinct roles.
- Pair format to visual: document with explainer, portrait with story, checklist with listicle,
  conversation with dialogue.
- Preserve the winner's broad beat logic only as scaffolding. Change the discovery mechanism and
  sentence construction.
- A different headline on identical body structure is not a new copy format.

## Headlines

- Front-load the useful idea.
- Match the creative's actual premise.
- Rotate question, notice, reassurance, curiosity, proof, and action-led shapes.
- Do not claim more than the approved primary text can support.
- Keep one headline from dominating a large batch unless it is an intentional control.

## CTA Shapes

- direct action
- private review or check
- learn more
- compare options
- answer a short question
- read the evidence
- see the next step

Use only CTAs that accurately describe the destination experience.

## QA

- Mark each draft with its format name.
- Compare openings side by side.
- Highlight repeated clauses, bullet stacks, proof order, and CTA wording.
- Verify every fact and claim through the campaign skill or approved source.
- Keep illustrative quotes clearly compliant; never fabricate customer or client testimony.
