# Women's-prison campaign — real testimonial research bank (compiled 2026-06-10)

Source material for CCWF/Chowchilla + CIW ad copy. All items below are from PUBLIC reporting/court records.
Personas remain fictional composites (disclaimer covers dramatization); never name victims; never name
perpetrators in ad copy (cite facts like "that officer got 224 years" without names).

## Hard facts usable as in-ad proof beats (verified mid-2026)

- **CCWF guard convicted Jan 2025** after 4-month trial — 64 counts (rape, rape under color of authority, etc.)
  against 9 women; 13 women testified, some still incarcerated, some in chains. **Sentenced Aug 2025: 224 years**
  (the max). First report against him was **2014** — that woman went to SHU and was transferred; case closed
  "insufficient evidence"; he continued ~8 more years. (Guardian, KQED, LA Times, Davis Vanguard)
- **Nearly 500 civil suits** filed re CA women's-prison staff abuse (CCWF + CIW) as of late 2025, growing weekly
  into 2026. No class action — individual claims. (Lawsuit Information Center, May–June 2026)
- **DOJ federal civil-rights investigation opened Sept 2024 into BOTH CCWF and CIW**, still open. (DOJ CDCA)
- **CIW convictions exist**: 2017 officers pleaded guilty (one entered a cell ~3:30am and groped two women;
  another forced oral sex in a cell — DNA preserved). 2012 officer pleaded guilty (impregnated a prisoner).
  (Press-Enterprise era coverage via Helping Survivors)
- CIW gyn-doctor federal suit (Feb 2025, 6 Jane Does): **alleged, no criminal charges** — women say they
  SKIPPED gynecological care entirely to avoid abuse. ⚠️ Keep ad framing generic ("medical staff"), never
  identify the role+facility+years combo (sole OB-GYN = identifiable). Firm sign-off needed for this angle.
- CDCR logged **800+ staff sexual-abuse complaints in 2022 alone**; hundreds since 2014 → only ~4 officers
  terminated. (Guardian data investigation)
- Statute context: AB 1455 (custodial claims vs officers, long windows), CCP 340.16, AB 2777 lookback
  **closes Dec 31, 2026** but does NOT revive claims against public entities; AB 250 window 2026–27 (cover-up
  required). ⚠️ Deadlines are case-specific — copy uses soft urgency only ("windows close"), never one hard date.

## Documented operational patterns (the relatability gold)

| Pattern | Detail from testimony |
|---|---|
| Fake call-outs | Women ducated to the **Board of Parole Hearings office** "about early release" — he was waiting. Blinds he'd peek through. |
| The nice one | "He was just a friendly face, always asking me how I was… I thought he cared." Gifts: gum, cosmetics, underwear, extra phone time (to a grieving woman). "We appreciate the nice staff… he positioned himself that way." |
| Parole leverage | His words per testimony: **"You don't want to be in that position because you want to go home."** "My date" = the universal fear. |
| Buddy system | Women organized never to be alone near certain officers. Housing-unit reaction to a first grope: "That's just him. The officers hide everything." |
| Reporting backfires | Reporter → SHU "for protection" → transferred away from kids → case closed. "I'm the alleged victim and you're treating me like a perpetrator." Victims 115'd for "inappropriate relationship"; STD-test request = admit "risky behavior" + possible added time. |
| Felon credibility | "We can't defend ourselves in here, because who is going to listen to us? We're a number in here." |
| Night shift (CIW) | 3:30am cell entries (pleaded guilty). Flashlight/count texture. |
| Skipped medical (CIW) | Women "chose to deprive themselves of gynecological care just to keep themselves safe." (alleged case — generic framing only) |
| Consent impossible | "The majority are not consensual… they can't say no" (HRW). CA law: prisoners cannot legally consent to staff. Many women self-disqualify as "it was a relationship" — biggest intake-expander insight. |
| Open secret | Other officers facilitated, intimidated victims, or propositioned the same women; "officers hide everything." |

## Authentic voice notes (from survivor speech across sources)

- Euphemism arc BEFORE the words "sexual abuse": "what he did", "what happened", "the situation",
  "messed with", "showed me attention", "played along". Mirror this arc — open with euphemism, name it by clip 3-4.
- Cadence: short declaratives; "I just…" constructions; present tense for past moments; ONE sensory detail
  (flashlight, blanket, shower, keys); anger aimed at the SYSTEM more than the man ("rotten to its core").
- Closing register of real survivors = duty, not catharsis: "I'm doing this for the women who are still inside."
- Insider terms confirmed in survivor speech: SHU, ad-seg/seg, write-up (115), 602, snitch, my date, the board,
  call-out/ducat, count, the yard, ISU, room search, "the police" (= guards). Budget 1-2 per ad.
- Facility naming: **"Chowchilla"** for CCWF. **"CIW"** for Chino — NEVER bare "Chino" (that's the men's
  prison, CIM). **"Frontera"** = what pre-2000s alumnae called CIW — instant age-cohort credibility unlock.
- Avoid: "rape" in the first beat, melodrama, any hint she "should have reported," victim self-pity.
